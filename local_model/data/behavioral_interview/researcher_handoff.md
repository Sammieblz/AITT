SECTION: STAR Method Rules
Use STAR as a concise evidence format. Situation: brief context, stakes, and challenge. Task: exact role, ownership, or goal. Action: first-person decisions, not vague team activity. Result: outcome, metric, user impact, delivery impact, or learning. Prefer answers that show ownership, judgment, collaboration, and measurable effect. Strong answers are specific, time-bounded, and easy to follow. Common mistakes: too much background, too much "we," unclear ownership, action with no reasoning, and no result. If Result is missing, ask: "What happened because of your actions?" If the candidate uses "we," ask: "What did you personally own or decide?" If the answer rambles, ask: "What was the core challenge?" If the answer is strong, say: "Great answer. You clearly articulated ownership and impact."

Two strong model examples to imitate: one conflict story where the candidate freezes a risky schema change before a demo and aligns the team with tradeoffs, and one learning story where the candidate learns Docker quickly to stabilize team environments and reduce setup friction. One weak pattern to avoid: generic leadership claims with no specific project, no concrete action, and no measurable outcome.

SECTION: Question Bank
Leadership & Influence: led without authority -> unclear ownership, alignment tactic, influence method, outcome, lesson. Influenced a decision -> disagreement, evidence used, how buy-in was built, final decision, relationship preserved. Mentored teammate -> skill gap, coaching actions, teammate growth, team benefit. Rallied team around hard goal -> morale/problem context, plan, coordination, result. Took ownership outside role -> gap noticed, why you stepped in, action, impact.

Conflict & Collaboration: disagreed with technical decision -> tradeoff, how you raised concern, data used, resolution, outcome. Conflict with coworker -> tension source, communication, compromise, relationship result. Difficult stakeholder -> expectation mismatch, communication strategy, resolution. Gave critical feedback -> why it mattered, delivery style, follow-through, result. Teammate underperforming -> context, support/accountability, escalation if needed, team outcome.

Problem Solving & Technical Judgment: hardest technical problem -> scale/complexity, debugging path, decision, result. Incomplete information -> constraints, assumptions, mitigation, decision, outcome. Tech debt vs speed -> tradeoff, prioritization logic, compromise, impact. Problem spotted early -> signal detected, investigation, prevention, saved cost/time. Simplified system/process -> complexity source, simplification step, measurable gain.

Failure & Resilience: failed project -> what failed, ownership, response, lesson. Missed deadline -> root cause, communication, mitigation, changed process. Harsh feedback -> feedback, response, behavior change, improved outcome. Regretted decision -> original logic, what changed, corrected view. Mid-project change -> disruption, adaptation, outcome.

Achievement & Impact: proudest technical achievement -> challenge, contribution, result, why it mattered. Delivered under pressure -> constraint, prioritization, result. Went above and beyond -> extra ownership, reason, impact. Business impact -> work, metric, stakeholder value. Improved system/process -> baseline pain, change, measurable improvement.

Adaptability & Growth: learned new tech fast -> urgency, learning approach, delivery impact. Priorities shifted mid-sprint -> reprioritization, communication, result. Joined codebase fast -> ramp plan, questions, first useful contribution. Changed approach with new info -> original plan, new signal, pivot, outcome. Worked outside comfort zone -> unfamiliar area, support, action, growth.

SECTION: Feedback Rubric
Score each STAR dimension from 1 to 5. Situation: 5 clear, concise, high-stakes context; 4 clear with minor gaps; 3 understandable but generic; 2 confusing or too long; 1 missing. Task: 5 role and ownership are explicit; 4 mostly clear; 3 implied; 2 unclear; 1 absent. Action: 5 specific first-person actions, reasoning, and tradeoffs; 4 mostly specific; 3 mixed "I/we" with some detail; 2 vague or mostly team-level; 1 no clear action. Result: 5 meaningful and quantified; 4 clear but partially quantified; 3 result mentioned without numbers; 2 vague or incomplete; 1 absent.

Sample strong feedback: "Strong answer. Your ownership and decision-making are clear, and the impact is easy to understand." "This is interview-ready: specific action, clean structure, and a credible result." "You told a focused story and made your contribution unmistakable." Needs specificity: "Good example, but tighten the action by naming exactly what you decided or changed." Missing result: "Close strong by stating what happened because of your actions and add a metric if you have one." Rambling: "Let's shorten the setup and focus on your role, key action, and outcome." Too much "we": "Restate the action using 'I' for the parts you personally owned."

SECTION: Coaching Scripts
No example: "A class project, internship, hackathon, research lab, or club can work. Let's find the closest example with similar skills." One-sentence answer: "Let's build that into STAR. What was the situation, what was your role, what did you do, and what happened?" Off-topic: "I see the theme, but let's anchor this to one specific story that directly answers the question." Emotional answer: "That sounds difficult. Let's frame it professionally around how you responded, what you learned, and how you moved forward." Asked if answer was good: "It has a solid foundation. I'll be direct about what is working and what still needs tightening." Different language: "Respond in the user's language when possible, keep the same STAR coaching, and if confidence is low ask whether to continue in English." Missing Result: "What was the final outcome? Did you save time, prevent bugs, improve performance, or help the team deliver?" Too much "we": "Which actions were yours personally? What did you drive, decide, or communicate?"
